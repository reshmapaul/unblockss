Medical records at your fingertips

A new three-way partnership between Unblock Health, Health Gorilla, and CLEAR® brings Individual Access Services (IAS) one step closer to reality.

Get medical records when you need them

Individual Access Services (IAS) used to feel like an impossible dream. But a new partnership between Unblock Health, Health Gorilla, and CLEAR is changing that.

It’s now possible for patients, providers, and caregivers to request digital health records from any organization and receive responses in as little as 90 seconds.

Imagine how healthcare will improve when…
  • Caregivers can share a loved one’s personal health records with their care team.
  • Home health providers understand a patient’s complete medical history.
  • Emergency physicians deliver care with all the information they need.

There’s still a lot of work to be done—and it will take time—but the technology is ready. The partnership between Unblock Health, CLEAR, and Health Gorilla makes all of this possible (and so much more).

The power of partnership for IAS

The Unblock Health platform gives patients a competitive advantage against their health conditions by reducing the administrative burden of requesting, receiving, and storing medical records. It’s like a “master key of patient access solutions.

CLEAR’s secure identity platform gives patients and providers better access and control of healthcare data. Their IAL-2 compliance meets the highest standard for protection and privacy, while a single verified identity reduces fraud and eliminates friction for users.

As a Qualified Health Information Network (QHIN) and interoperability solution, Health Gorilla provides the technology and network for organizations to share patient information quickly and securely under the Trusted Exchange Framework and Common Agreement (TEFCA).

How can you help move IAS forward?

The IAS technology works. The partnership between Unblock Health, Health Gorilla, and Clear can get the records you need in as little as 90 seconds.

But there’s a problem. Not enough organizations have currently opted in to TEFCA, which means there aren’t enough organizations responding to IAS requests (yet).

You can help change that.

By creating an account and making a records request, you can help show policymakers how much demand there is for mandate enforcement.

The more pressure we place on healthcare organizations to share medical records, the faster we’re likely to see enforcement begin.

Request medical records in 3 easy steps

Step 2
